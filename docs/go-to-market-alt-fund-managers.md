## 1 · ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)

The sweet spot: **boutique LU firms with 5–30 staff that prepare tax
returns for alt-fund managers**, OR **small-to-medium fund managers
(€50M–€1B AUM) with a lean back-office** that outsource compliance to
those firms.

Two customer shapes to target in parallel:

### Primary — Boutique tax / fiduciary firms
- **Size**: 5–30 headcount. Big enough to have systemic pain with
  Excel, small enough to buy without a 3-month procurement cycle.
- **Pain signals**: Excel-driven ops, recurrent AED penalties, staff
  turnover breaks institutional knowledge, partners stuck reviewing
  low-level work.
- **Decision-maker**: Managing Partner (single signer), usually also
  the senior tax technician. No procurement dept.
- **Budget**: €5–20k ARR — the "nice expense" range. Paid annually.
- **Examples**: think NOOS Partners, Fiduciaire Tessin, Cobel Group
  (these are real LU boutiques; illustrative — not necessarily targets).

### Secondary — In-house tax ops at mid-size fund managers
- **Size**: €50M–€1B AUM, 10–40 staff.
- **Fund structures**: AIF, SIF, RAIF, SICAR, Securitisation vehicles,
  Holding SOPARFIs. The more structures, the more compliance.
- **Pain signals**: growing entity count (10→50), Head of Compliance
  job is chased by deadlines, 1 hire away from paying €60k for
  another ops person — €15k SaaS looks like a bargain.
- **Decision-maker**: CFO or Head of Compliance.
- **Budget**: €10–30k ARR.
- **Less common as buyers today** — they mostly outsource to firms
  in the primary ICP. But the compound story (cifra replaces an
  ops hire) resonates with growing managers.

### Who to avoid (for now)
- **Big 4 (KPMG / EY / Deloitte / PwC LU)** — they have internal
  tools, 12-month buying cycles, and in-firm politics. Revisit at
  Series A.
- **Single-person shops (1 lawyer + 1 admin)** — can't afford €5k/yr.
- **Banks / UCITS management companies** — different vendor
  procurement, regulatory scrutiny that adds 6 months.

### Where they cluster (geography)
- **Kirchberg quarter in Luxembourg City** — 80% of targets within
  walking distance of each other. Café density is the unsung
  advantage: one morning can hit 3 coffees without driving.
- **Windhof / Contern / Findel office parks** — secondary pockets.
- Some firms have London / Geneva / Madrid satellites for international
  fund origination; the LU head office is still the buyer.

## 2 · Channels, ranked by effort/ROI

### 🟢 Highest ROI — warm intros via professional networks (LPEA / ALFI / AIMA)

**Why**: LU is small (~600k people, finance sector ~50k). Diego has
been practising here for years; 2 degrees of separation from any
target. Partners talk to partners. This is the #1 acquisition channel
at early stage.

**How**:
- **Map the graph**: list every acquaintance who works at a firm in
  the ICP. Use LinkedIn 1st-degree connections as seed. Target 40–60
  warm intros as the pipeline base.
- **Don't ask for a pitch call — ask for a coffee**. The frame is
  "I'd love your feedback on what I'm building; you've seen the Excel
  pain yourself". That gets ~80% acceptance where a pitch would get
  20%.
- **Bring the product**: open `/tax-ops` on the laptop at the table.
  Show the matrix. Show the Excel import. Show the deadline alerts.
  5 minutes of demo beats 30 minutes of slides.
- **Convert in 2 moves**: after the coffee, send a 2-paragraph email
  summarising what cifra does + one sentence pricing + "can I
  onboard you with your 2026 data for free for 2 months?". Low-
  commitment pilot.

**Expected conversion**: 40–50% of warm coffees → pilot; 30–40% of
pilots → paid customer. 10 warm coffees = ~3 paying customers.

### 🟢 High ROI — LPEA / ALFI / Finanzplatz events

LPEA (Luxembourg Private Equity Association) and ALFI (Association
of the Luxembourg Fund Industry) run regular events:
- **LPEA Annual Meeting** (Oct) — ~400 attendees, €500 ticket, half
  are decision-makers.
- **LPEA Insights** (quarterly, 1-hour panels + cocktails) — free,
  ~80 attendees, intimate.
- **ALFI London Conference** (Nov) — UK fund distributors who sell
  into LU; reach beyond LU borders.
- **ALFI Private Assets Conference** (June) — alt-fund focus, exact
  target segment.
- **Finanzplatz Luxembourg** events — broader but higher volume.

**Tactic**: pick ONE event per quarter, show up 30 min before doors
open, don't hide in the programme — circulate at the cocktail. Aim
for **5 quality intros per event** (name, card, reason to follow up
within a week). Book the 5 follow-up coffees in the calendar
before leaving.

**ROI**: ~5 quality leads per event, ~2 coffees booked, ~1 pilot
started per event. €500 ticket + half-day time = ~€1000 cost, ~€10k
ARR per pilot. Net positive.

### 🟡 Medium ROI — LinkedIn content + Sales Navigator DM

**Content** (published under Diego's personal LinkedIn):
- **Cadence**: one post per 2 weeks. More than that burns authority
  on low-value stuff.
- **Format**: 200–400 words, specific LU tax angle (e.g. "The 2024
  C-288/22 TP ruling means natural-person directors are not taxable
  persons — here's what that changes for VAT recovery on director
  fees"). Always with a CJEU citation. Diego's technical depth is
  the moat — use it.
- **Visual**: one screenshot from `/tax-ops` or `/declarations` per
  post. Concrete > abstract.
- **CTA**: "If you're preparing these for alt funds and want to
  compare notes, DM me." Soft, not pushy.

**Sales Navigator DMs**:
- **50 targets / month**, identified via filters: location=Luxembourg,
  job title in (Tax Partner, Head of Compliance, Managing Director,
  CFO) at firms with 5–50 employees in categories (Tax Services,
  Investment Management, Financial Services).
- **Template** (3 sentences):
  > Hi {first_name}, noticed {specific_detail_from_their_profile}.
  > I'm building cifra — a LU tax compliance workspace that replaces
  > the Excels for CIT + VAT + sub-tax + WHT across entities. Would
  > you have 20 min next week to compare notes on how your firm handles
  > the deadline tracking today?
- **Follow-up**: 7 days later if no reply, one more message referencing
  a specific piece of value ("saw you posted about the March 2026
  AED circular — the admin tolerance change affects us too; happy to
  share how we modelled it"). Then stop — two touches max, no nagging.

**ROI**:
- Content: slow compounder; 6–12 months to see inbound inquiries,
  but the inquiries that come are hot ("your LinkedIn post on X
  made me realise we need help").
- DMs: 15–25% reply rate from warm targets, 2–5% from cold; of
  replies, 20–30% convert to a meeting. 50 DMs/month = ~2 meetings
  booked.

### 🟡 Medium ROI — Targeted podcasts + press appearances

- **Luxfinance Daily** (EY's weekly podcast) — occasionally interviews
  founders in LU fintech. Not easy to get but high credibility.
- **LuxTimes / Paperjam interviews** — practitioner profiles. Pitch
  after shipping a "milestone" (first 10 customers, cross-border
  expansion, etc).
- **Property Law Luxembourg** (niche podcast) — adjacent audience.

**Tactic**: don't cold-pitch. Ship product milestones, then offer
the story. Journalists love "founder building vs Big 4" narratives.

**ROI**: 1 podcast/interview per quarter, each generates ~5 inbound
DMs, ~1 converted.

### 🔴 Low ROI — Cold email

Don't invest here until all the above are saturated. LU is too
small; most targets know each other or have a 2nd-degree connection
to Diego. Cold emails feel lazy where a warm intro is cheaper.

**Exception**: London / Geneva / Madrid fund managers who have LU
entities but no LU office — warm intros harder. A 5-email cold
sequence may land 1 meeting per 100 emails. Do this only after
stabilising LU revenue.

## 3 · Templates

### LinkedIn DM — initial touch (3 sentences)

```
Hi {first_name}, noticed {specific_personal_detail}.

I'm building cifra — a LU tax compliance workspace that turns your
CIT / VAT / WHT / subscription tax Excels into a live dashboard with
deadlines, status per entity, and one-click year rollover.

Would you have 20 min next week to compare notes on how you handle
deadline tracking today? Always useful to hear.
```

Variant for someone publicly lamenting AED delays / compliance
pain:
> Hi {first_name}, saw your post on {topic}. Totally relate — I'm
> building a LU tax compliance workspace for exactly this. 20 min
> next week to compare notes?

### LinkedIn DM — 7-day follow-up (2 sentences)

```
Hi {first_name}, following up — also just shipped {concrete_recent_change}
in cifra that might be relevant given {their_context}. Happy to share
the demo either way.
```

### Cold email — subject + 5-line body

Subject: `{their firm}: the LU tax Excel replacement`

```
{first_name},

I'm the founder of cifra, a LU tax compliance workspace I built after
8 years drafting CIT + VAT returns myself (used to rebuild the
148-entity Excel every January like most of the boutique firms do).

What it does: one matrix per tax type (CIT / VAT / sub-tax / WHT /
BCL) with live deadline alerts respecting AED admin tolerance,
auto year rollover, inline edit, xlsx export per view.

I'm in the beta phase offering 3 months free to 5 LU firms who'll
give structured feedback. Your firm came to mind because of
{specific_reason}.

Open to a 20-min call next week?

Diego
```

### Referral ask (to a current customer or supportive acquaintance)

```
Hi {first_name}, if it ever comes up naturally — if you're chatting
with someone at a LU firm that still runs the Excel compliance
machine — I'd love an intro. Cifra is in its first 10-customer
push; warm intros are our single highest ROI channel.

No pressure if nothing comes to mind — but "Amanda at X firm
mentioned she was fighting deadlines last month" is exactly the
hook I need.
```

### Follow-up sequence (3 touches, 7 / 14 / 30 days)

**Touch 1 (Day 7)**: value reference — a relevant change, ruling,
or article. "Thought of you re: {thing}. Happy to chat if useful."

**Touch 2 (Day 14)**: different angle — pitch a specific mini-demo.
"Would a 10-min walkthrough of the VAT quarterly matrix be useful?
That's where I see the most Excel pain at firms your size."

**Touch 3 (Day 30)**: break-up message. "Moving you off my follow-up
list — happy to revisit later in the year. Meanwhile, here's a
doc with the full playbook + screenshots in case it's useful:
{link}."

## 4 · Weekly cadence (realistic for Diego)

Diego is a non-technical founder with limited evening hours + two
small kids. Cadence must be sustainable for 12+ months. Proposed:

| Activity | Weekly | Monthly total | Time budget |
|----------|--------|---------------|-------------|
| Outbound DMs (LinkedIn) | 10 | ~40 | ~1h/week |
| Warm coffees in Kirchberg | 2 | ~8 | ~2h + prep/follow-up |
| LinkedIn Pulse article | 0.25 | 1 | ~2h/month total |
| LPEA / ALFI event attend | 0.25 | ~1 | ~4h/event |
| Pipeline review in `/crm/outreach` | 1 | ~4 | 30min Monday morning |
| Customer discovery interview | 1 | ~4 | ~45min + notes |

**Total weekly commitment**: ~5–6h/week sustained. Front-load the
week: Monday = pipeline review + DMs; Tue-Wed = coffees; Thu = content
(if the week); Fri = follow-ups. Avoid weekend sales work — will burn out.

## 5 · Benchmark metrics — early-stage B2B SaaS, LU tax niche

Expected numbers at month 6 in steady state. These are benchmarks
for Diego to track against, not targets set in stone.

| Metric | Benchmark | Source |
|--------|-----------|--------|
| Warm DM reply rate | 15–25% | B2B founder averages + LU network density |
| Cold DM reply rate | 2–5% | Industry standard, cold LinkedIn |
| DM → meeting booked | 20–30% | B2B SaaS, high-trust niche |
| Meeting → proposal sent | 40–50% | Technical product, qualified leads |
| Proposal → close | 30–40% | Long sales cycle (3–6 months) |
| Pilot → paid conversion | 60–80% | If pilot is well-scoped and used |
| Average ACV (annual contract value) | €8–15k | Boutique firm budget |
| Month 6 ARR target | €30–60k | 3–5 paying customers |
| Month 12 ARR target | €100–180k | 8–12 paying customers + expansion |
| Customer acquisition cost | €500–1500 | Per paying logo (mostly time) |
| Payback period | 4–8 months | Reasonable for SMB SaaS |

**If after 6 months metrics are 50% below benchmark**: re-evaluate
ICP or channels. Likely the pitch isn't landing or targets are wrong.

**If metrics are 50% above benchmark**: don't get cocky. Ride it.
Ship more features, double outbound velocity.

## 8 · Anti-patterns to avoid

1. **Pitching in the first 30 seconds of a coffee**. Listen first, pitch
   only if prompted. Most coffees convert because Diego LISTENED, not
   because he PITCHED.

2. **"We're not just X, we're Y also"** scope-creep in pitch. cifra
   starts with "LU VAT compliance" — finish that sentence before
   layering on Tax-Ops + ViDA + Peppol + DAC6. Don't boil the ocean
   in a 20-min intro.

3. **Free tier temptations**. Don't offer a free tier. Offer a 3-month
   paid pilot at 50% off. Free → Diego has no customer urgency to
   ship, the "customer" has no urgency to provide feedback.

4. **Over-customization for the first customer**. Say "v2" when a
   boutique asks for their specific workflow quirk. Only build it when
   2+ customers ask independently.

5. **Ignoring the Excel export feature in demos**. Every customer
   who saw Excel export in the stint-36 demo asked "so I can still
   share with my auditor?". Make sure it appears in the first
   3 minutes of the live demo.
